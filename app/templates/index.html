Success Story

Lepisi is a 34-year-old married woman with five children. She runs a business of preparing and selling barbecue in her community to earn a living. Lepisi has three years of experience in this business. She is a Samoan citizen and lives in a rural area. Lepisi works very hard to please her customers. With a total loan of $775, she takes her business to the next level. She plans to use her profits to build a small restaurant and to pay for her children’s education.

Dreams Realized

Nazira runs a small sewing shop that employs three local women in Bokonbaevo, Kyrgyzstan. In her shop, Nazira handcrafts traditional women’s clothing and felt souvenirs using natural, locally-sourced materials and patterns inspired by tribal traditions. Nazira uses a loan of $8,000 to invest in new equipment for her sewing shop, employing at least 4 additional women and selling her products in stores across Kyrgyzstan.

An Example For All

Ricardo is 69 years old and did not have the opportunity to receive formal education. He is married and has three children who are now independent. Ricardo works in agriculture and raising cattle. He currently has five cows and two bulls. He also processes the milk they provide in order to sell dairy products every morning. Ricardo uses the loan to buy commercial feed for his animals. He previously received two loans and paid them off satisfactorily. His goal is to continue investing in cattle as it is his primary source of income. In this way he hopes to generate greater income for his family.
